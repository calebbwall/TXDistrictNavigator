## Next: importing Android contacts into the app (important reality check)

You can absolutely add a contact import flow, but there’s a catch: **Android contact “Notes” are notoriously not exposed reliably through common RN/Expo contact libraries**. The Expo Contacts docs exist, but multiple reports show the **note field coming back empty on Android**. ([Expo Documentation][1])

So the most dependable approach for your use case (single-user app for your dad) is usually one of these:

1. **VCF (vCard) import** (recommended)

* Export contacts on Android to a `.vcf` file (includes notes much more often than API reads).
* In the app: “Tools → Import Contacts” → pick `.vcf` → parse → map into your app’s “private notes” fields.

2. **Google Contacts API sync**

* If the contacts are Google-synced, you can OAuth and pull contact notes from Google’s side (more setup, but very robust).

If you want the “upload contact data from Android into the app” to be painless and reliable, **VCF import is the quickest win**. After you confirm you’re good with VCF-based import, I’ll give you a Replit prompt that adds:
